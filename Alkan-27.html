ALKAN-27

Sound-Based Waste Sorting Mechanism. The system rewards users with monetary incentives for depositing aluminum cans, promoting recycling and increasing accessibility and efficiency, particularly in remote areas.

Role: lead engineer, programmer, and team manager

Skills: Prototyping, Design, Coding, Project Management, Arduino

Problem
Purpose
Objective
Limited nearby recycling facilities pose a challenge for aluminum can recycling. The lack of easy access to these centers results in improper disposal of aluminum cans in regular waste bins, impeding effective waste management and hindering the efficient reuse of aluminum. This has a negative impact on the environment and our recycling efforts.
Address the problem of limited or lack of nearby recycling facilities and promote effective aluminum can recycling, facilitate proper sorting and recycling of aluminum cans, reduce waste and minimize environmental impact, and provide an accessible and cost-effective solution, encouraging sustainable recycling practices and maximizing the reuse of valuable aluminum resources.
Our objective is to develop an affordable device that utilizes sound signatures to distinguish aluminum cans from waste. The device should stay within a budget of $500 while maintaining a minimum success rate of 50% in accurately identifying aluminum cans. It should also accommodate various sizes of aluminum cans, enabling efficient recycling practices.

Design Process

To understand potential reasons for low recycling rates, I observed my community and identified two potential factors:

Waste categorizer: a device that automatically categorizes waste into recyclables, landfill, compost, hazardous waste, etc., to save time.

Aluminum deposit: a device that serves as a place for people to deposit aluminum cans and receive monetary incentives.

To better understand the reasons behind low recycling rates, I conducted a global survey. Surprisingly, the primary cause was the lack of access to nearby recycling facilities. This finding led us to choose the second idea.

Incentive Process

Prototype 1

Demo

The mechanism analyzes the sound of the can hitting the plate. If it detects an aluminum can, the plate pivots towards the recycling side, activating the coin dispensing system for users to collect coins. If the item is not an aluminum can, the plate pivots towards the trash side and does not activate the coin dispensing system.

Test Results

Tests were conducted using various objects (aluminum cans, Styrofoam cups, etc.). The prototype performed better when the mechanism was outside the box. This could be attributed to factors such as heat accumulation, sensor sensitivity, and sound variations within the enclosure. The second prototype underwent modifications to address these issues.
